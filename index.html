Introduction

YouTube, currently the second most visited website in the world, is a video site that provides an easy way for ordinary computer users to upload videos. With the development of technology, broadband and the popularization of photographic equipment, short film information is very popular. With its simple interface, YouTube allows any video uploaded to the network to be watched by the world audience in a few minutes. This enables netizens to change from traditional information receivers to information publishers.

Function

Netizens can set up their own private theaters, video publishing stations, and news stations. The rise of video sites has largely played the role of broadcast media's next leader, and YouTube as the most influential video site shows us how online media is about to become the new mainstream media.

Changes

Over the course of YouTube's rise, it has gone through numerous optimization updates. By learning and adapting popular models from other online social platforms, YouTube has also added many new features to its website, including ads, membership, streaming, real-time chat room (interaction), YouTube Shorts, etc. Since 2005, it has taken ten years of evolution for YouTube to become at the top of the industry today. Let's see how YouTube became a success in the new era of communication media.
Youtube Timeline

February 2005

YouTube was founded by Chad Meredith Hurley, Steve Chen and Jawed Karim.

April 23, 2005

Co-founder Karim released a 19-second video titled "Me at the Zoo." It was the first video on YouTube and the starting point for YouTube to become a milestone in the development of Internet culture in the 21st century. The Los Angeles Times commented on the film: "As the first video uploaded to YouTube, it played a pivotal role in fundamentally changing the way people use media and ushering in a new golden age of one-minute shorts" . As of November 23, 2021, "I'm at the Zoo" has been viewed 200 million times and left 11.01 million comments. Google bought YouTube in 2006 for $1.65 billion in stock.

The summer of 2006

YouTube had grown into one of the fastest-growing sites on the World Wide Web, with 4,000 short videos and beyond 6 million visitors a day. It was ranked as the fifth most popular website by Alexa, even surpassing MySpace's growth rate at the time. YouTube automatically recommends videos to its users, and as of early 2019, 70% of the videos its users watched came from automated recommendations. YouTube makes full use of the advantages of video and audio media, covering entertainment music, social networking, popular culture, film and television, games, education, publicity and other markets, and creating online creation and related markets of YouTube industry.

July 17, 2015

YouTube had well over 1 billion users, and almost a third of all people in the world who use the Internet spend hundreds of millions of hours on YouTube every day.
